Iconic Mid‑Century Chairs

A curated selection of the most emblematic chair designs from the golden age of modernism — timeless silhouettes, organic materials, and the spirit of innovation.

1. Eames Lounge Chair (1956)

Designed by Charles & Ray Eames, this lounge chair perfectly blends comfort with craftsmanship. Molded plywood, rich leather, and a silhouette that became a symbol of modern luxury.

2. CH24 Wishbone Chair (1949)

Hans J. Wegner’s iconic chair is inspired by Ming Dynasty furniture. Its Y‑shaped back and paper‑cord seat make it both sculptural and inviting.

3. Panton Chair (1960)

Verner Panton created the first fully plastic, single‑mold chair. Its bold curves and futuristic form define the playful, experimental side of mid‑century modernism.

4. Barcelona Chair (1929)

Ludwig Mies van der Rohe designed this chair for the German Pavilion at the Barcelona International Exposition. Sleek, architectural, and eternally elegant.

5. Egg Chair (1958)

Arne Jacobsen designed the Egg Chair for the SAS Royal Hotel in Copenhagen. Its enveloping shape creates a sense of privacy and comfort while remaining stylish and modern.
